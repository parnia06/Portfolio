Roasted Carrots

Wash them well (no need to peel!). Then, cut the carrots crosswise into 2-inch barrels.
Slice any very thick barrels in half vertically. This will help them cook at the same
rate as slimmer pieces. Place the chopped carrots in a large bowl. Toss them with the honey,
olive oil, salt, and pepper, mixing well to evenly coat them. Spread them in a single layer on
a sheet pan lined with parchment paper. Transfer to a 425°F oven and roast for 15 to 25 minutes.
The carrots are ready when they’re fork-tender and nicely browned, but not mushy.

Buttery Cooked Carrots

Gather all ingredients. Cook carrots in a large pot of boiling water until tender, about 8 to 10 minutes.
Strain carrots, leaving just enough of the cooking water to cover the bottom of the pan; set carrots aside.
Stir in brown sugar and butter; simmer and stir until butter melts. Return carrots to the pot and toss to coat.
Cover and let sit for a few minutes to allow flavors to mingle.
